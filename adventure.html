Guidelines & Information

General Adventure Guidelines

  • Always follow instructions from your guide or instructor
  • Inform us about any medical conditions before booking
  • Wear appropriate clothing and footwear for the activity
  • Stay hydrated and protect yourself from the sun
  • Respect local customs, traditions, and the natural environment
  • Travel insurance with adventure activity coverage is mandatory
  • Book activities at least 48 hours in advance
  • Arrive 15 minutes before the scheduled start time

What to Bring

  • Valid identification (passport or ID card)
  • Copy of your travel insurance policy
  • Personal medications if required
  • Water bottle and snacks
  • Sunscreen, hat, and sunglasses
  • Camera (waterproof for water activities)
  • Cash for additional expenses or tips

Trekking Guidelines

  • Acclimatize properly to avoid altitude sickness
  • Trek at your own pace - it's not a race
  • Stay on marked trails to avoid getting lost
  • Carry enough water and purification tablets
  • Pack light but include essential gear
  • Inform someone about your trekking plans
  • Be prepared for sudden weather changes
  • Respect wildlife and maintain distance

Recommended Gear

  • Sturdy hiking boots with ankle support
  • Layered clothing (base layer, insulation layer, outer shell)
  • Warm hat, gloves, and thermal socks
  • Sleeping bag rated for cold temperatures
  • Trekking poles for stability
  • Headlamp or flashlight with extra batteries
  • First aid kit with blister treatment
  • High-energy snacks and water bottles

Rafting Safety Guidelines

  • Always wear the provided life jacket and helmet
  • Listen carefully to the safety briefing before starting
  • Learn and understand the paddling commands
  • Stay in the raft unless instructed otherwise
  • If you fall out, float on your back with feet forward
  • Keep hands and feet inside the raft when navigating rapids
  • Hold the paddle with one hand on the T-grip at all times
  • Inform your guide if you feel uncomfortable or unsafe

What to Wear for Rafting

  • Quick-drying clothes (shorts/swimwear and t-shirt)
  • Secure footwear that won't come off in water (not flip-flops)
  • Sunscreen (waterproof recommended)
  • Strap for glasses or contact lenses
  • Change of dry clothes for after the activity
  • Towel and toiletries
