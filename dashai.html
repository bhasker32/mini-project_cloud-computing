Dashain & Tihar Festivals

Experience Nepal's most vibrant and culturally significant festivals that bring families together in celebrations lasting for weeks.

Overview

Dashain and Tihar are Nepal's two biggest festivals, celebrated annually with immense enthusiasm. Dashain spans 15 days in September/October, followed by Tihar (the festival of lights) about two weeks later. Both festivals hold profound religious and cultural significance for Nepali people, celebrating the victory of good over evil and bringing families together in joyous celebrations.

When to Experience

  • Dashain: September-October (during Ashwin/Kartik months in Nepali calendar)
  • Tihar: October-November (follows Dashain by about two weeks)
  • Duration: Dashain (15 days), Tihar (5 days)

The exact dates vary each year as they are determined by the lunar calendar. These festivals transform Nepal into a vibrant display of traditions, with streets and homes decorated with colorful lights and ornaments.

Cultural Heritage

Cultural Significance

These festivals represent the victory of good over evil and are times for family reunions, receiving blessings from elders, and indulging in festive foods and activities. They strengthen family bonds and community ties while preserving ancient traditions.

Dashain Festival

Dashain celebrates the victory of Goddess Durga over the demon Mahishasura, symbolizing the triumph of good over evil. It's the longest and most anticipated festival in Nepal.

15-Day Celebration

The 15 Days of Dashain

Each day of Dashain has its own significance and rituals. The most important days are Ghatasthapana (first day), Fulpati (seventh day), Maha Ashtami (eighth day), Maha Navami (ninth day), and Vijaya Dashami (tenth day). Families reunite, homes are cleaned and decorated, and special meals are prepared throughout the festival period.

Dashain Activities & Traditions

Kite Flying

Colorful kites dot the sky during Dashain, symbolizing messages to the gods. It's believed that flying kites brings prosperity and helps guide departed souls to heaven. Children and adults alike enjoy this tradition, with neighborhoods coming alive with kites of various colors and designs.

Ping (Bamboo Swings)

Temporary bamboo swings called "ping" are constructed in villages. These tall swings represent the connection between heaven and earth. Young people gather around these swings, waiting for their turn to soar high into the sky, symbolically reaching toward heaven.

Card Games

Families and friends gather to play cards during the festive period. Gambling is traditionally permitted during Dashain as it's believed to please Goddess Laxmi. These gatherings are not just about games but also serve as a time for sharing stories and strengthening bonds.

Tihar Festival

Also known as Deepawali or the Festival of Lights, Tihar is celebrated for five days with lights, colors, and prayers to different deities. Houses glow with oil lamps, candles, and electric lights.

Festival of Lights

The 5 Days of Tihar

Each day of Tihar is dedicated to worshipping different deities: crows, dogs, cows, oxen, and brothers. Houses are decorated with oil lamps, candles, and colorful rangoli patterns. The festival illuminates homes and hearts, creating a magical atmosphere throughout Nepal.

Tihar Activities & Traditions

Rangoli (Aipan)

Beautiful designs made with colored rice, flour, sand, or flower petals decorate entrances to welcome the goddess Laxmi. These intricate patterns symbolize prosperity and spiritual connection, featuring geometric designs, flowers, and auspicious symbols.

Deusi Bhailo

Groups go door-to-door singing traditional Deusi and Bhailo songs, offering blessings to households in exchange for money, food, or treats. These cultural performances bring communities together and spread joy throughout neighborhoods.

Lighting Diyo

Oil lamps (diyo) illuminate houses, creating a magical atmosphere across neighborhoods and symbolically guiding Goddess Laxmi to blessed homes. The warm glow of these lamps transforms cities and villages into enchanting displays of light and hope.

Festival Foods

These festivals are incomplete without special delicacies prepared to celebrate the occasion. Families spend days preparing traditional dishes that are shared with relatives and neighbors.

Dashain Specialties

Khasi ko Masu (Mutton Curry)

Spicy slow-cooked goat meat curry, a must-have during Dashain celebrations. This rich dish is prepared with aromatic spices and often served with rice or traditional Nepali bread.

Sel Roti

Ring-shaped sweet rice bread, deep-fried and crispy on the outside, soft inside. This traditional bread is made from soaked rice flour mixed with sugar, butter, and spices, then formed into circular shapes and fried.

Aloo Tama

Potato and bamboo shoot curry with black-eyed beans, tangy and flavorful. This unique combination creates a distinctive taste that has been part of Nepali cuisine for generations.

Mithai (Sweets)

Various traditional sweets including laddu, barfi, and jeri made from milk, sugar, and nuts. These colorful confections are essential during Tihar and are offered to deities before being shared with family and guests.

Shakarpara

Deep-fried sweet pastry made from flour, sugar, and ghee, popular during Tihar. These diamond-shaped treats have a delightful crunch and subtle sweetness that makes them perfect for festive occasions.

Samaybaji

Newari festival platter with beaten rice, spiced meat, boiled egg, soybeans, and more. This elaborate dish represents the rich culinary heritage of the Newari community and showcases a perfect balance of flavors and textures.

Religious Significance

Beyond the celebrations and feasts, these festivals carry deep spiritual meaning and reinforce cultural values that have been passed down through generations.

Hindu Mythology

Dashain

Dashain celebrates the victory of Goddess Durga over the demon king Mahishasura. The festival symbolizes the triumph of good over evil. The first nine days represent the battle between Durga and Mahishasura, while the tenth day marks the victory.

People visit temples dedicated to Goddess Durga, particularly Shaktipeeths (places sacred to the divine feminine). Animal sacrifices are traditionally performed to honor the goddess, though this practice is declining in urban areas.

The goddess Durga, depicted with multiple arms holding various weapons, represents divine feminine power that protects the universe from evil forces and maintains cosmic order.

Divine Worship

Tihar

Tihar honors various deities and aspects of life. The worship of animals acknowledges their contribution to human life and their spiritual significance. The festival culminates in celebrating the brother-sister relationship.

The most important ritual is Laxmi Puja, dedicated to the goddess of wealth and prosperity. Homes and businesses are thoroughly cleaned and decorated with oil lamps to guide Laxmi to bestow blessings of prosperity.

The worship of animals during Tihar reflects Nepal's holistic worldview that recognizes the interconnectedness of all living beings. Even household items and vehicles are adorned with flowers and blessed during this period.

"These festivals are not just religious ceremonies but serve as occasions for family reunions, renewal of community ties, and expressions of gratitude to deities, animals, and family members who make life prosperous and meaningful. They represent the living heritage of Nepal that continues to evolve while preserving ancient traditions."

Tips for Travelers

If you're planning to experience these magnificent festivals in Nepal, here are some practical suggestions to help you make the most of your visit.

Cultural Etiquette

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples or attending ceremonies
  • Always remove shoes before entering homes or temples
  • If invited to a home celebration, bringing sweets or fruits is appreciated
  • Ask permission before photographing ceremonies or people
  • When receiving tika, accept it with both hands as a sign of respect

Planning Your Visit

  • Book accommodations at least 2-3 months in advance
  • Expect transportation delays during peak festival days
  • Many businesses close during major festival days
  • Consider visiting Kathmandu Durbar Square during Dashain
  • Plan to stay through both festivals for the complete experience
  • Pack comfortable walking shoes for temple visits

Best Places to Experience

  • Kathmandu Valley temples for authentic Dashain rituals
  • Thamel area during Tihar evening illuminations
  • Patan Durbar Square for traditional celebrations
  • Bhaktapur for authentic cultural experiences
  • Pokhara for a more relaxed festival atmosphere
  • Local villages for genuine rural celebrations
